Vision

To promote holistic development from the individual to the nation by nurturing ethically grounded, value-driven, and socially responsible citizens through character building, ethical leadership, and transformative development, thereby contributing to sustainable social and economic progress and inclusive nation-building.

Mission

The Mission of the Trust is to empower Indian citizens and grassroots communities through comprehensive and inclusive education by fostering ethical leadership, advancing research in sustainable development, and delivering ICT-enabled capacity-building initiatives. The Trust shall function as a strategic bridge between research and policy, translating evidence-based knowledge into actionable solutions, in collaboration with Government bodies, Non-Governmental Organizations, and other stakeholders, for the effective implementation of impactful programmes in health, education, skill development, sustainable development, and livelihood enhancement, thereby contributing to the creation of a resilient, informed, and self-reliant nation.

Core Values

Value Statement of Glo-CED

The Value Statement of Glo-CED defines the core principles that guide our organization's behaviour and decision-making. We stand on these four pillars:

Integrity & Ethical Leadership

We hold ourselves to the highest ethical standards, ensuring that our leadership and programs serve as a trusted model for the communities and individuals we empower.

Evidence-Based Impact

We are committed to rigorous research and data-driven solutions, ensuring our programs in health, education, and livelihood are both effective and sustainable.

Inclusivity & Empowerment

We believe in the inherent potential of every citizen and prioritize grassroots participation to ensure that development is equitable and reaches the most marginalized.

Collaborative Innovation

We embrace the power of ICT and strategic partnerships with government and NGOs to create scalable, forward-thinking solutions for a resilient nation.

Our Charter

Objects of the Trust

Document No. 302 of 2025 of Book IV

a.

To establish, promote, and support educational and capacity-building initiatives in the fields of ethics, values, leadership, character and personality development, and development studies for the benefit of students, youth, and the general public.

b.

To undertake, promote, and support research, studies, documentation, consultancy, and impact assessment in areas including the Indian economy, poverty alleviation, livelihoods, health, education, climate change and environment, human resource development, Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), social innovation, social entrepreneurship, and gender issues, in association with government bodies, academic institutions, and voluntary organisations.

c.

To organise and facilitate as a Centre for Networking with Development Professionals and Experts in conducting Seminars, Workshops, Webinars, Conferences, Trainings and publications in creating awareness and sensitizing the Academia, Students, Youth, interested Citizens, Media, and Policy Makers.

d.

To design, implement, and support need-based welfare and development programmes in the areas of ethics and values, soft skills and skill development, health, education, livelihoods, environment, disaster management, leadership, and human resource development, and to act as a facilitating bridge between grassroots communities and policy-level institutions for the empowerment of children, youth, women, senior citizens, tribal and marginalised communities, and persons with disabilities with the support of Government agencies, Non-Government agencies, Corporates and other sectors.

e.

To train, empower, and build the capacities of local communities through the use of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) for sustainable development and social transformation.

f.

To study, document, preserve, promote, and disseminate Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) for the benefit of society, academia, and policymakers.

g.

To establish, manage, and strengthen grassroots training centres for developing barefoot communities into self-reliant and resilient communities by providing access to skills, information, and appropriate technologies.

h.

To prepare, publish, and disseminate training modules, curricula, Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) materials, journals, reports, books, and other knowledge resources for education, research, and public awareness.

i.

To collaborate and cooperate with central and state governments, statutory authorities, universities, autonomous institutions, and other agencies for programme implementation, research, policy advocacy, and institutional development.

j.

To design and offer certificate programmes, micro-credential courses, and digital badges in emerging and socially relevant areas, in accordance with applicable academic and regulatory guidelines.

k.

To promote volunteerism, field-based education, experiential learning, internships, and cultural exchange programmes for students, youth, and community members.

l.

To provide care, counselling, rehabilitation, and referral services for victims of crime and other vulnerable groups, in collaboration with appropriate government and non-government agencies.
